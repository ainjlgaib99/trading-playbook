MORNING ROUTINE
What you do before 6:30 AM — every single day
⏰ Start This 45 Minutes Before 6:30 AM
No social media. No financial news. No distractions. Your mind needs to be clear before you look at price.
1
Open TradingView — 30 min chart on NQ or ES. Look at the overnight session. Where did price go? Did it make new highs or new lows? Where did it settle? The overnight range shows you where institutions were positioning.
2
Pull your Fixed Range Volume Profile (FRVP) from yesterday's RTH session — 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM close. Mark three horizontal lines: VAH (top of value), VAL (bottom of value), POC (fattest node = most volume). These are your levels for the day.
3
Check the economic calendar for news events. If CPI, FOMC, NFP, or any major release is scheduled at or before 8 AM — you close the platform and come back tomorrow. News destroys your levels.
4
Mark yesterday's high and yesterday's low as additional reference lines. Price gravitates to these too.
5
At 6:30 AM — watch only. Do not touch anything for the first 15 minutes minimum. Let the market show you where it wants to go before you commit capital.

02 / 10
PICKING LEVELS
How to build your map the night before
After 4 PM when RTH closes, open TradingView. Your job takes 10 minutes. Do this every trading night — it becomes automatic.
1
Set your Fixed Range Volume Profile from 9:30 AM open to 4:00 PM close (today's RTH session). You'll see the full volume distribution.
2
Identify and write down three numbers:
VAH — top of the value area (where 70% of volume was traded)
VAL — bottom of the value area
POC — the single highest-volume price level (fattest bar)
3
Mark today's high and today's low as horizontal lines. These are tomorrow's reference points too.
4
On Mondays and Fridays — also pull the weekly FRVP. Weekly VAH and VAL are even stronger magnetic levels than daily ones.
The Core Principle
Price does not move randomly. It auctions up to find sellers and auctions down to find buyers. VAH is where sellers were active. VAL is where buyers were active. POC is where the most agreement happened. Tomorrow price will return to test these levels again. That's your edge.
The rule: You only take trades when price is at or very near one of your marked levels. Away from levels = no trade. If price is in the middle of the value area, you are watching, not trading.
03 / 10
THE ENTRY
What a perfect setup looks like — step by step
There are two primary setups. Both follow the same logic: wait for the level, wait for the second tap, wait for confirmation. You are never entering on the first touch.
Setup 1 — VAH Rejection (Short)
Price rallies into VAH from below. First touch = do not enter. Price pulls back, then comes back up for a second tap. On the second touch, you see a rejection candle — wick rejection, bearish engulfing, momentum slows. That is your entry. Short. Stop above VAH. Target POC then VAL.
Setup 2 — VAL Bounce (Long)
Price drops down into VAL from above. First touch = do not enter. Price bounces, then comes back down for a second tap. On the second touch, you see a bullish rejection — hammer candle, buyers stepping in. That is your entry. Long. Stop below VAL. Target POC then VAH.
1
Price approaches your level. You are watching, not touching.
2
First touch occurs. Price reacts. You note it. Still watching.
3
Price pulls back away from the level. Good — this validates the level.
4
Second tap — price returns to the level. Now you are focused.
5
Confirmation candle forms — rejection wick, momentum shift, change in character. This is your trigger.
6
Before entering — calculate your RR. If stop to target is not at least 1:2, you skip it. No exceptions.
7
Enter. Set stop. Set target. The trade is now managed by your rules, not your emotions.
❌ Never Do This
Do not enter on the first touch. Do not enter in the middle of the value area. Do not enter without a confirmation candle. Do not enter if RR is less than 1:2.
04 / 10
TRADE MANAGEMENT
What you do after you're in — three rules only
01
Breakeven Rule
Once price moves 1R in your direction — move stop to entry. You cannot lose this trade. Lock it in.
02
Partial Exit
At first target (1R or POC), take 50% off the position. Let the rest run to full target. Secure profit, ride the rest.
03
Chop Exit
If price stalls mid-range and starts chopping sideways, close it. A small win beats a winner turned loser.
❌ The One Rule You Never Break
Never move your stop further away because "it might come back." Your original stop was your maximum risk. Moving it away is how small losses become account-killing losses. The market does not care about your feelings.
Once you're in a trade, there are only three things you can do: hold, take partial, or close. That's it. There is no "let me see what happens" — that's how you lose. If you don't have a plan before you enter, you don't enter.

WHEN TO STAY OUT
Sitting on your hands is a trade
The best traders sit on their hands 80% of the time. Staying out when conditions are bad is how you protect your account. Every single item below means zero trades today.
✕
Major news event within 30 minutes of your session — CPI, FOMC, NFP, jobless claims. News makes your levels meaningless.
✕
Price is in the middle of the value area — no edge here, pure chop. You only trade at levels, not away from them.
✕
You already took 2 trades today — doesn't matter if they won or lost. 2 trades is your daily limit. Close the platform.
✕
You already lost twice today — hard stop, no exceptions. You are done. Walk away. No revenge trading.
✕
You are emotional — angry from a loss, overconfident from a win, distracted, tired, or in a bad headspace. Emotions cost money.
✕
Overnight range is extremely wide (100+ points on NQ) — the market is already volatile and your levels are less reliable.
✕
You don't have a clear level to trade from — if you are guessing where to enter, you are out. No guessing.
The Right Mindset
You do not need to trade every day to make money. A week with 4 good trades and 0 bad ones beats a week with 15 trades and blown risk. Patience is literally the edge.
06 / 10
PASSING EVALS
Topstep · Apex · Lucid — how to not blow it
The eval is not testing if you can make big money. It is testing if you can follow rules consistently. That is a completely different game than just trading.
⚠️ #1 Reason Evals End
Hitting the daily loss limit. A $50K account might have a $2,000 daily loss limit. Lose $1,500 and keep trading, lose another $600 — failed. Walk away when you're down 50–60% of the daily limit. That's your personal daily stop, not the firm's limit.
1
Trade smaller than you're allowed. The eval lets you trade 5 contracts. Trade 1. Your job is to prove consistency, not max P&L.
2
Never hit the daily loss limit. Set your own personal daily stop at 50–60% of the firm's limit and respect it like a hard rule.
3
Take your time. Most firms require 10–15 minimum trading days. There is no rush. Take 2–3 high quality trades per week. Slow and clean.
4
Treat every day as independent. A bad Monday does not affect Tuesday. The market doesn't owe you a recovery. Start fresh.
5
Most people fail in the first week by trying to make it all back after a loss. You will not do this. You walk away after 2 losses and come back tomorrow.

COMMON MISTAKES
What beginners do wrong with volume profile
✕
Trading the first touch. First touch at VAH or VAL is discovery — price is just testing the level. You wait for the second tap. Always.
✕
Using too short a timeframe for the profile. A 2-hour profile means nothing. Use the full prior RTH session (9:30 AM – 4:00 PM) at minimum.
✕
Treating POC as a hard reversal. POC is a magnet — price gravitates to it — but price often passes right through it. It is a target, not always an entry.
✕
Ignoring overall market context. If the market is in a strong uptrend, VAH rejections fail constantly. Know if you are in a trending or balancing market before taking counter-trend trades.
✕
Taking every touch regardless of RR. You only trade setups that give you at least 1:2. If the target is too close or the stop is too wide — skip it. Doesn't matter how "perfect" it looks.
✕
Revenge trading after a loss. You lost. It happens. The market didn't do it to you personally. Walk away for the day and come back tomorrow.
✕
Not journaling. If you can't look at 3 months of data and see a clear pattern of what's working — you haven't been honest in your entries.

RISK RULES
The math that keeps you in the game
These are not suggestions. Breaking risk rules is the single fastest way to blow an account or fail an eval.
Standard Risk Per Trade
$200–250. On a $50K Topstep eval the max drawdown is ~$2,500. Lose 2 trades at $250 each = $500 down. Still well within limits. Still calm. Still able to recover without emotion.
Max Risk — High Conviction Only
$500 maximum. Only on the clearest, cleanest setups — maybe one per week qualifies. Not every trade is $500. Most are $200–250.
How to calculate your stop in ticks:
MNQ — $2 per tick
$200 risk ÷ $2/tick = 100 ticks stop distance
$250 risk ÷ $2/tick = 125 ticks stop distance
$500 risk ÷ $2/tick = 250 ticks stop distance
MES — $5 per tick (0.25 points)
$200 risk ÷ $5/tick = 40 ticks stop distance
$250 risk ÷ $5/tick = 50 ticks stop distance
$500 risk ÷ $5/tick = 100 ticks stop distance
Hard Rule
If your stop requires more ticks than your risk allows — the setup is not valid. Skip it. Do not widen your risk to fit the trade. Let the trade pass.

YOUR TIMELINE
Honest expectations — no shortcuts
Consistently profitable trading takes 6–18 months of serious, focused practice. Anyone telling you less is selling you something.
MONTH 1–3
Paper trading only. Learn to see levels. Learn why you were right or wrong after every trade. Most people skip this because it feels fake. Don't. This is where your foundation is built. Journal every single paper trade as if it were real money.
MONTH 3–6
Small live account or eval account. Real money introduces emotions you cannot simulate on paper. You will discover psychological issues you didn't know you had. Anger. FOMO. Hesitation. These will show up now. Learn from them.
MONTH 6–12
Consistency starts appearing. You'll see a clear edge — certain setups you win on consistently. Others you still struggle with. Start cutting the setups you consistently lose on. Double down on what's working.
MONTH 12–18
Real consistency. If you've journaled everything and made real adjustments based on data, this is when it comes together. Not every month is green, but you have a repeatable edge and the discipline to execute it.
The Real Filter
The people who make it are the ones still showing up in month 8 after a rough stretch. Most quit in month 3. Consistency in showing up is more important than any setup.
